How Aquaponics Works

Aquaponics is a **closed-loop system** where plants and fish thrive together.

  • Fish Waste → Provides nutrients for plants.
  • Plants → Filter and clean water for fish.
  • Recycling → Minimal water usage & sustainable farming.

Benefits of Aquaponics

  • ✅ **Water Efficient** – Uses 90% less water than traditional farming.
  • ✅ **Chemical-Free** – No need for fertilizers or pesticides.
  • ✅ **High Yield** – Grow vegetables 2x faster than soil farming.
  • ✅ **Sustainable** – Self-sustaining ecosystem with **minimal waste**.

How to Set Up an Aquaponics System

Follow these **simple steps** to create your own aquaponics system:

  1. Choose a Location: Place your system in a **sunlit** area.
  2. Setup a Fish Tank: Use a **200-500L tank** for fish.
  3. Add a Grow Bed: Place plants **above the fish tank**.
  4. Install a Water Pump: **Circulates** water between fish & plants.
  5. Select Fish & Plants: Tilapia, catfish, lettuce, and basil work best.
  6. Monitor the System: Check **pH, ammonia & nitrate** levels regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ What fish can I use in aquaponics?

Tilapia, catfish, koi, and goldfish are commonly used.

❓ Do I need to change the water?

No, the system naturally recycles water. Just **top up** when needed.

❓ Can I use any plants?

Leafy greens like lettuce, spinach, and herbs grow best.
