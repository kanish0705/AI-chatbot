What is Aquaponics?

Aquaponics is a combination of aquaculture (raising fish) and hydroponics (growing plants without soil). The fish provide nutrients for the plants, and the plants help clean the water for the fish â€” a perfect, sustainable cycle.

How It Works

Water from the fish tank is pumped to the plant beds. Beneficial bacteria convert fish waste into nutrients for the plants. The clean water is then cycled back to the fish tank. This system conserves water, requires no soil, and produces both plants and fish.

Benefits of Aquaponics

  • Uses 90% less water than traditional farming
  • Produces organic vegetables and fresh fish
  • Requires less space and no soil
  • Promotes sustainable food production
