What is Aquaponics?

Aquaponics is a combination of aquaculture (raising fish) and hydroponics (growing plants without soil). The fish provide nutrients for the plants, and the plants help clean the water for the fish ‚Äî a perfect, sustainable cycle.

Types of Aquaponics Systems

  • Media Bed System: Uses gravel or clay pebbles as a growing medium.
  • Nutrient Film Technique (NFT): Water flows in shallow channels for plant roots.
  • Deep Water Culture (DWC): Plants float on rafts in nutrient-rich water.

How to Set Up Your Aquaponics System

  1. Choose a location (backyard, greenhouse, rooftop).
  2. Set up a fish tank and fill it with dechlorinated water.
  3. Install grow beds above or beside the tank.
  4. Add pumps, pipes, and aeration systems.
  5. Introduce bacteria to convert fish waste into nutrients.
  6. Add fish and plants after 3-4 weeks of cycling.
